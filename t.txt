1-The Regulating Act of 1773, an Act of the Parliament of Great Britain, limited the influence of the Governor-General of India and established the Council of Four, elected by the East India Company's Court of Directors
2-Pitt's India Act of 1784 reduced the membership to three, and also established the India Board
3-During the rule of the East India Company, the council of the Governor-General of India had both executive and legislative responsibilities
4-The council had four members of the Council elected by the Court of Directors
5-The first three members were permitted to participate on all occasions, but the fourth member was only allowed to sit and vote when legislation was being debated
6-In 1858, the British Crown took over the administration from the East India Company
7-The council was transformed into the Imperial Legislative Council, and the Court of Directors of the Company which had the power to elect members of the Governor-General's Council ceased to have this power
8-Instead, the one member who had a vote only on legislative questions came to be appointed by the Sovereign, and the other three members by the Secretary of State for India
